# MolTrace — ROI Methodology

## A defensible measurement protocol for converting order-of-magnitude claims into tenant-specific evidence

---

## Why this document exists

The headline ROI numbers in the MolTrace white papers — *"5–10× compression of time-to-structure"*, *"$300K/year recouped FTE cost"*, *">98 % reproducibility rate"* — are order-of-magnitude estimates derived from internal validation runs and community-reported baselines. Procurement and finance teams reasonably ask: **"How do we measure this in our own environment?"**

This document defines:

1. The four quantitative outcome metrics MolTrace tracks
2. The measurement protocol for each metric (how to collect baseline + post-deployment data, instrumented inside the platform)
3. The statistical-significance bar a tenant should hold the data to before reporting
4. A fill-in template the pilot team can complete during the standard 30-day pilot

The methodology is deliberately conservative — it counts only the time / cost components that are directly attributable to MolTrace deployment, excludes savings that the tenant cannot easily defend to an auditor, and uses tenant-supplied hourly costs rather than industry averages. The output is a defensible internal ROI memo, not a marketing artifact.

---

## 1. The Four Outcome Metrics

### Metric 1 — Hours saved per analysis (HSPA)

**Definition.** Median analyst time consumed per complete structure-elucidation analysis, baseline minus post-deployment.

**Components measured.** Peak picking, integration adjustment, candidate ranking, evidence-narrative drafting, internal review, regulatory-affairs handoff prep.

**Components excluded.** Sample preparation, instrument acquisition time (acquisition is unchanged by MolTrace), final human-review time (held constant by tenant policy), unrelated administrative overhead.

**Unit.** Hours per analysis, median across the pilot's analysis set.

**Baseline collection.** Pre-deployment: ten consecutive representative analyses, analyst-self-reported time per task, normalised against tenant's task-time-tracking system if one exists.

**Post-deployment collection.** First ten consecutive MolTrace-completed analyses, same task list, same self-reporting protocol.

**Default headline claim from MolTrace (order-of-magnitude).** 4–10 hours saved / analysis for mid-complexity small molecules. **Measured tenant value goes here.**

### Metric 2 — Time-to-dossier (TTD)

**Definition.** Calendar elapsed time from first raw-data upload of an analytical batch to release of the regulatory-affairs-ready report.

**Components measured.** Analyst processing time, internal cross-check time, regulatory-affairs review-and-edit time, signoff queue time.

**Components excluded.** Time spent waiting on external regulators, time spent on parallel batches.

**Unit.** Calendar days, median across the pilot's batch set.

**Baseline collection.** Six representative batches from the twelve months pre-deployment, drawn from the tenant's existing project-management system.

**Post-deployment collection.** Six MolTrace-completed batches, same selection criteria, same data source.

**Default headline claim.** 60–80 % reduction. **Measured tenant value goes here.**

### Metric 3 — Reproducibility rate (RR)

**Definition.** Fraction of analyses whose final report can be regenerated from the raw archive + recipe + input parameters in < 5 minutes by a second reviewer.

**Components measured.** Re-execution of MolTrace analysis pipeline on the original raw archive with the recorded recipe hash; comparison of regenerated report's structured JSON to the original.

**Components excluded.** Non-MolTrace analyses (no fair comparison without an equivalent baseline reproducibility protocol).

**Unit.** Percentage (regenerated reports identical to original) of attempted regenerations.

**Baseline collection.** Tenant supplies its current best-effort reproducibility estimate against the same definition, drawn from a sample of ten arbitrarily-selected historical analyses (typical industry baseline: 70–90 %).

**Post-deployment collection.** Twenty MolTrace-completed analyses regenerated by a second reviewer using the platform's `re-run-from-archive` workflow.

**Default headline claim.** > 98 %. **Measured tenant value goes here.**

### Metric 4 — Audit cycle time (ACT)

**Definition.** Median analyst + regulatory affairs time consumed per inspection-style question ("show me the raw data that produced this number").

**Components measured.** Time from question-receipt to defensible answer-delivery, including any necessary re-execution, evidence-package assembly, and reviewer-summary generation.

**Components excluded.** Question-formulation time, question-routing time (no fair comparison).

**Unit.** Hours per question, median across a five-question audit-drill set.

**Baseline collection.** Pre-deployment: five mock inspection questions answered against tenant's existing data-management system. Tenant analyst records start-to-finish time per question.

**Post-deployment collection.** Same five mock inspection questions answered against MolTrace's UI traceback + audit-event ledger + export package.

**Default headline claim.** Days → minutes per question. **Measured tenant value goes here.**

## 2. Statistical-Significance Bar

For each metric, the tenant should not report a savings until:

- **HSPA** — ten baseline + ten post-deployment analyses collected; median difference ≥ 1 hour and p < 0.05 by a paired Wilcoxon signed-rank test.
- **TTD** — six baseline + six post-deployment batches collected; median difference ≥ 30 % and a one-sided p < 0.10 (lower bar acceptable due to small sample).
- **RR** — twenty MolTrace regenerations attempted; success rate with 95 % Wilson confidence interval reported.
- **ACT** — five questions per condition; report median + range rather than a point estimate.

These bars are deliberately stricter than typical marketing-grade reporting. A pilot that fails to clear them should not be reported as a successful ROI case, regardless of headline impression. The MolTrace pilot-success-management team will help the tenant interpret data that does not clear the bar — usually the path forward is a second-cohort run with a refined task list.

## 3. Fully-Loaded Hourly Cost (FLHC)

The dollar-denominated ROI claim multiplies hours saved by FLHC. Use the tenant's own FLHC, not an industry benchmark. The tenant's FLHC for an analytical chemist typically includes:

- Base salary
- Employer payroll taxes
- Benefits (health, retirement, insurance)
- Allocated overhead (facilities, equipment, IT, management)

US pharma analytical-chemist FLHC typically falls in the **$120 – $200 / hour** range as of 2026. Choose the tenant's audited number from their FP&A system. If FP&A cannot supply a number, use **$150 / hour** as a conservative working assumption and flag this in the final memo.

## 4. Annual ROI Calculation

```
Recouped FTE cost ($) = N_analyses_per_year × HSPA × FLHC

Where:
  N_analyses_per_year = tenant-supplied count
  HSPA                = measured hours saved per analysis (Metric 1)
  FLHC                = tenant-supplied fully-loaded hourly cost (§3)
```

This is the headline number for the tenant's internal ROI memo. It is conservative because:

- It counts only **direct analyst time savings** (Metric 1)
- It excludes the harder-to-quantify but typically larger savings in **regulatory affairs cycle time** (Metric 2) and **audit response cost** (Metric 4)
- It uses the tenant's audited FLHC, not an industry average

For a defensible upper bound on the ROI, add:

```
Recouped RA cycle cost ($) = N_dossiers_per_year × TTD_baseline_days × RA_FLHC × TTD_reduction_fraction × 8 hr/day
Recouped audit cost ($)    = N_audit_questions_per_year × (ACT_baseline_hours − ACT_post_hours) × FLHC
```

Sum the three components for a "total recouped cost" number, but report each separately so the reviewing finance team can adjust the assumptions.

## 6. Reporting the Result Back

When the template is complete, the tenant should produce two artifacts:

**Artifact 1 — Internal ROI Memo (1–2 pages).** Renders the template above in narrative form for the tenant's internal finance / procurement review. Includes the per-metric measured number, the statistical-significance status, the dollar-denominated annual ROI, and the recommendation.

**Artifact 2 — MolTrace Case-Study Submission (optional, anonymised).** With the tenant's permission, MolTrace will publish the measured values (anonymised) in a public case-study addendum to the white papers. This replaces the order-of-magnitude headline numbers in the white papers with measured cross-tenant aggregates. Tenants who consent to publication receive a named or anonymised attribution per their preference.

## 7. Common pitfalls

**Pitfall 1 — Sample selection bias.** Baseline analyses tend to be picked for tractability; post-deployment analyses tend to be the new exciting work. Mitigation: select both cohorts from the same project category, same complexity tier, same SOP. The MolTrace pilot team can help define a fair selection criterion.

**Pitfall 2 — Hawthorne effect.** Analysts move faster when they know they're being measured. Mitigation: collect baseline before announcing the pilot timeline; collect post-deployment data 14+ days into the pilot (after the novelty has worn off).

**Pitfall 3 — Cherry-picked saving channels.** Reporting only Metric 1 (HSPA) without Metrics 3 (reproducibility) and 4 (audit) inflates the apparent saving. Mitigation: always report all four metrics, even if some don't clear the significance bar.

**Pitfall 4 — Conflating MolTrace with adjacent process changes.** A tenant adopting MolTrace often simultaneously tightens its SOP, hires a new analyst, or upgrades instrumentation. Mitigation: hold all adjacent changes constant during the pilot window, or explicitly call out the confounders in the memo.

**Pitfall 5 — Mismatched FLHC.** Using a US national-average FLHC for a tenant operating in a low-cost geography (or vice versa) skews the dollar number. Mitigation: use the tenant's audited FLHC from FP&A, not a public benchmark.

## 8. The Five-Question Audit Drill

The Metric 4 audit drill uses a standard five-question set designed to exercise the typical FDA / EMA inspection style. The questions:

1. *"Show me the raw bytes that produced the integration value reported in row 7 of the candidate-comparison table."*
2. *"What processing recipe was used to produce the spectrum shown in §3.2 of the dossier? Who approved that recipe?"*
3. *"Reproduce the candidate ranking against the same raw data. Does the ranking match the dossier? If not, why?"*
4. *"For the proton at 5.30 ppm, what literature citation justifies its categorisation as anomeric rather than olefinic?"*
5. *"List every audit event for this sample between [date X] and [date Y]. Who reviewed, who released, who exported?"*

A pre-deployment baseline analyst should answer these against the tenant's existing tooling; a post-deployment analyst answers them against MolTrace. Both are timed start-to-finish.
